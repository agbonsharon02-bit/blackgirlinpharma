Medical Affairs

The scientific bridge between R&D and commercial — where you influence medicine, shape policy, and directly impact patient care without ever losing your scientific identity.

01

What Is Medical Affairs?

Medical Affairs (MA) is the function that ensures the scientific integrity of a company's drug development and commercial activities. MA professionals — primarily Medical Science Liaisons (MSLs), Medical Directors, and publication specialists — serve as the trusted scientific voice both internally and externally.

They build relationships with Key Opinion Leaders (KOLs), support evidence generation, lead medical education, and provide scientific guidance to commercial and regulatory teams. Unlike sales, MA is non-promotional; their conversations with HCPs are scientific and data-driven.

In Canada, Medical Affairs operates under Innovative Medicines Canada (IMC) guidelines for scientific exchange, ensuring that all interactions with HCPs remain compliant, evidence-based, and clearly separated from promotional activities. MSLs working in the Canadian market cover territory across all provinces and frequently collaborate with provincial health authorities and regional formulary decision-makers.

Medical Affairs sits at the intersection of science, strategy, and relationship — a rare combination that makes it one of the most intellectually rich and professionally rewarding careers in all of pharma.

Quick Fact

MSLs spend the majority of their time in the field engaging directly with physicians, researchers, and academic medical centers — discussing clinical data, pipeline updates, and scientific findings in a peer-to-peer, non-promotional capacity. This is as close to academia as you can get inside industry.

MA professionals work closely with clinical development, commercial, regulatory, and market access teams — making this one of the most cross-functional and strategically influential roles in the pharmaceutical industry.

02

Why Choose Medical Affairs?

If you have a doctoral degree and a passion for science, patient outcomes, and relationship-building, Medical Affairs offers a career that keeps you at the frontier of medicine — without the constraints of a lab bench or a sales quota.

Scientific Depth

You stay close to cutting-edge science throughout your entire career — discussing published data, pipeline innovations, and real-world evidence with the world's leading clinicians.

KOL Access

You build relationships with the world's leading physicians, researchers, and academic thought leaders — a professional network that compounds in value over your entire career.

Respected Autonomy

MA operates independently from sales — no quotas, no promotional messaging. Your credibility is built on science, and your conversations are guided by data and clinical need.

High Compensation

MSLs and Medical Directors are among the highest-paid roles in pharma — without requiring ongoing lab-bench research. Base salaries start well above $100K for field-based positions.

03

Skills You'll Need

A strong Medical Affairs professional blends deep scientific expertise with interpersonal intelligence, strategic thinking, and the ability to communicate complex data in ways that resonate with expert clinicians. The science is table stakes — what differentiates great MA professionals is how they apply it.

Scientific Communication

Translating complex clinical and preclinical data into clear, credible scientific narratives for HCP audiences at all levels of expertise.

KOL Relationship Management

Building and sustaining long-term, trust-based relationships with Key Opinion Leaders, investigators, and academic medical centers in your therapeutic area.

Clinical & Medical Literature Literacy

Critically appraising published studies, understanding study design strengths and limitations, and staying current with emerging evidence in your therapeutic area.

Health Economics & Outcomes Research (HEOR)

Understanding health technology assessments, patient-reported outcomes, and real-world evidence to support value demonstration and market access discussions.

Publication Planning & Medical Writing

Developing and executing publication strategies that accurately and compliantly communicate clinical data through peer-reviewed journals, congresses, and medical education programs.

Cross-Functional Strategic Thinking

Partnering with commercial, regulatory, market access, and clinical teams to align medical strategy with broader business and patient access objectives.

Bonus Skill

Proficiency in real-world evidence (RWE) analysis and familiarity with health technology assessment (HTA) processes are rapidly becoming differentiators for Medical Affairs professionals at senior levels.

Certifications That Open Doors

Medical Affairs is a doctoral-degree-heavy field — most MSL roles require a PharmD, MD, PhD, or equivalent clinical/research doctorate. Beyond your degree, the following credentials signal professional commitment and deepen your competitive edge.

Most Recognized

MSL Certification (CMSL)

Offered by the MSL Society, the CMSL is the most widely recognized credential for practicing Medical Science Liaisons. It validates field-based MA expertise through exam and demonstrated experience.

Career Pathway

Medical Affairs offers a structured and well-compensated career ladder. Most professionals enter as MSLs after completing a doctoral degree, then advance into regional leadership, strategic medical director roles, and ultimately executive medical affairs leadership. Here is what the trajectory typically looks like:

1

Entry Level · 0–3 Years

Medical Science Liaison (MSL) / Associate MSL

Build KOL relationships in an assigned territory, deliver scientific presentations, support clinical trial site identification, and gather field medical insights to inform internal strategy.

$110K–$130K CAD Doctoral degree required Field-based KOL engagement
2

Mid-Level · 3–7 Years

Senior MSL / Regional Medical Lead

Lead a regional MSL team, serve on advisory boards, contribute to medical strategy planning, and act as a scientific resource for cross-functional commercial and regulatory teams.

$130K–$162K CAD CMSL recommended Territory leadership Advisory boards
3

Senior Level · 7–12 Years

Medical Director / Associate Medical Director

Drive pipeline strategy and publication planning, serve as the medical lead for a therapeutic area, interface with executive leadership, and provide scientific direction to the broader organization.

$198K–$238K CAD Pipeline strategy Publication planning Executive interface
4

Leadership · 12+ Years

VP Medical Affairs / Chief Medical Officer

Own the full medical affairs strategy for a portfolio or enterprise. Lead MSL teams, publication programs, and medical education initiatives — and represent the company's scientific voice at the board level.

$238K–$305K+ CAD Portfolio strategy Board-level representation Team leadership

Pro Tip

Many MSLs come from clinical practice (pharmacists, physicians, nurses) or academic research. If you have a doctoral degree and patient-facing or research experience, you are already well-positioned — the scientific training is the hardest part to build. The transition to industry is more achievable than most people think.

06

Interview Tips

MSL and Medical Affairs interviews are uniquely rigorous — they test your scientific knowledge, communication ability, and interpersonal style simultaneously. Here is how to walk in prepared:

01

Prepare a Scientific Slide Presentation

Most MSL interviews include a mock KOL detail or journal club presentation. Practice presenting a key paper in your therapeutic area clearly, confidently, and conversationally — not like a lecture, but like a peer discussion.

02

Know the Promotional vs. Non-Promotional Distinction

Understand the difference between promotional and non-promotional scientific exchange — this is a compliance-critical distinction in Medical Affairs. In Canada, this boundary is governed by Health Canada's guidelines on direct-to-consumer advertising and the IMC Code of Ethical Practices. Be ready to articulate how MSLs stay within compliant scientific exchange boundaries under these frameworks.

03

Go Deep on the Therapeutic Area

Research the therapeutic area thoroughly — oncology, rare disease, neurology, immunology, etc. Speak the language of the clinicians you'd be engaging. Know the standard of care, unmet needs, and key clinical trials in the space.

04

Demonstrate Relationship-Building Stories

MSLs win on trust, not data dumps. Come prepared with specific examples of how you have built and maintained meaningful professional relationships — whether with faculty, patients, or colleagues. Show that you listen as much as you speak.

05

Know the Company's Pipeline and Published Data

Review the company's ClinicalTrials.gov listings, key publications, and any recently approved indications before your interview. Being able to speak fluently about their lead asset signals genuine preparation and scientific interest.

06

Ask Thoughtful Questions About Medical Strategy

Ask questions that signal strategic thinking: "How does MA feed into the label strategy?" or "What does your real-world evidence program look like?" or "How are MSL insights incorporated into publication planning?" These show you think beyond the field role.

Growth Outlook

Medical Affairs is one of the fastest-growing functions in pharma. As pipelines become more scientifically complex — cell therapy, gene editing, precision oncology — companies are investing heavily in MSL teams and medical strategy. Canada's biopharmaceutical sector is expanding rapidly, with particular growth concentrated in Ontario, Quebec, and British Columbia. According to Innovative Medicines Canada, member companies continue to increase investment in field medical teams and evidence generation, creating sustained demand for skilled MA professionals across the Canadian market at every career level.

Resources to Get Started

You don't need to navigate this journey alone. These are some of the most helpful organizations, platforms, and communities for anyone building a career in Medical Affairs:

MSL Society (themsls.org)

The premier community and certification body for MSLs globally — widely used by Canadian Medical Affairs professionals.

MAPS (medicalaffairs.org)

Medical Affairs Professional Society — training programs, certification, global events, and industry standards for MA professionals.

Innovative Medicines Canada (innovativemedicines.ca)

Canada's research-based pharma industry association. Essential for understanding the IMC Code of Ethical Practices governing Medical Affairs activities in Canada.

Health Canada Drug Product Database (canada.ca)

Browse approved Canadian drug products, labels, and regulatory history — essential for MSLs staying current on their products.

PubMed (pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov)

Stay current with clinical literature in your therapeutic area — the primary tool for tracking evidence you'll discuss with KOLs.
