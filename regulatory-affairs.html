01

What Is Regulatory Affairs?

Regulatory Affairs (RA) is the function within pharmaceutical, biotechnology, and medical device companies responsible for ensuring that products are safe, effective, and compliant with all applicable laws and regulations before and after they reach the market.

Think of RA professionals as the bridge between science and government. They prepare and submit the documentation that regulatory agencies — like Health Canada, the European Medicines Agency (EMA), and other international regulators — require to approve a new drug, biologic, or device. In Canada, this culminates in a Notice of Compliance (NOC), the formal approval granted under the Food and Drugs Act. They also monitor ongoing compliance once a product is on the market.

Without Regulatory Affairs, no medicine ever makes it to a pharmacy shelf. That's how central this role is to the entire pharmaceutical ecosystem.

Quick Fact

Health Canada processes hundreds of drug submissions annually — from New Drug Submissions (NDS) to Clinical Trial Applications (CTA) — each one prepared by regulatory affairs professionals working behind the scenes to ensure the data package is complete, accurate, and meets Health Canada's Therapeutic Products Directorate (TPD) and Biologics and Genetic Therapies Directorate (BGTD) standards.

RA professionals work closely with research scientists, clinical teams, quality assurance, medical affairs, and legal — making this one of the most cross-functional and intellectually stimulating careers in the industry. No two days look the same.

02

Why Choose Regulatory Affairs?

If you love science but also thrive in strategic thinking, writing, problem-solving, and collaboration, Regulatory Affairs might be your perfect fit. Here is why so many pharma professionals choose this path — and stay.

High Earning Potential

Salaries range from $58K CAD at entry level to well over $175K CAD at the director and VP level — with strong bonuses in large pharma.

Global Opportunities

Every country with a pharmaceutical market needs RA professionals. Your skills are transferable across Canada, the US, EU, UK, and beyond.

Job Security

Regulations don't disappear. As long as companies make medicines, they need RA experts. It is one of the most stable functions in pharma.

Real Impact

You are directly responsible for getting life-saving medicines to patients. That's a tangible, meaningful contribution you'll feel every day.

03

Skills You'll Need

A strong RA professional combines scientific literacy with strategic thinking and exceptional attention to detail. You do not need to be a chemist or clinician — but you do need to understand scientific data and communicate it clearly to regulators.

Regulatory Writing

Drafting clear, accurate submission documents — CTAs, NDS, CTDs — in the format regulators expect.

Regulatory Intelligence

Tracking changes in Health Canada, ICH, and international guidelines so your company stays ahead of evolving requirements.

Scientific Literacy

Understanding preclinical and clinical data, pharmacology basics, and how to interpret study results.

Cross-Functional Collaboration

Working effectively with clinical, CMC, safety, marketing, and legal teams to compile complete dossiers.

Project Management

Managing multiple submission timelines simultaneously, prioritizing deadlines, and coordinating stakeholders.

Agency Communication

Preparing for and participating in meetings with Health Canada, EMA, and other agencies. Understanding what they're looking for and why.

Bonus Skill

Proficiency in eCTD (electronic Common Technical Document) publishing tools like Veeva Vault, DocuBridge, or similar platforms is increasingly valued — and easy to learn on the job or through free online tutorials.

Certifications That Open Doors

While a science degree is often the minimum foundation for RA roles, certifications can dramatically strengthen your application — especially if you are transitioning from a different pharma function or entering the field without prior RA experience.

Most Recognized

RAC (Regulatory Affairs Certification)

Offered by RAPS (Regulatory Affairs Professionals Society), the RAC is the gold standard credential in the field. Available in US, EU, and Canadian streams.

Offered ByRAPS
Experience Needed3+ years RA work
FormatWritten exam
Entry-Friendly

RAPS Fundamentals Certificate

A great starting credential for newcomers. Covers the basics of drug development, regulatory submissions, and how agencies work — no prior RA experience required.

Offered ByRAPS
Experience NeededNone required
FormatOnline courses
Industry Staple

GxP / GMP Training Certifications

Understanding Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP), Good Clinical Practice (GCP), and Good Laboratory Practice (GLP) is essential for any RA role. Many providers offer short online courses.

Offered ByVarious (TOPRA, PAREXEL)
Experience NeededNone required
FormatOnline / self-paced
Advanced Path

M.S. in Regulatory Affairs

Several universities now offer Master's degrees specifically in Regulatory Affairs, providing deep expertise in submissions strategy, labeling, pharmacovigilance, and global regulations.

Notable ProgramsTemple, Johns Hopkins, NEIU
Duration1–2 years
FormatOnline & in-person options
05

Career Pathway

Regulatory Affairs offers a clear and rewarding career ladder. Most professionals enter at the associate or specialist level, then build expertise over time to move into management, strategy, and leadership roles. Here is what a typical trajectory looks like:

1

Entry Level · 0–2 Years

Regulatory Affairs Associate / Coordinator

Support senior RA staff with document preparation, publishing submissions, tracking agency responses, and maintaining regulatory databases. This is where you learn the craft.

$58K–$78K CAD BS/MS degree Document management eCTD basics
2

Mid-Level · 2–5 Years

Regulatory Affairs Specialist

Lead individual submission projects, write regulatory sections, manage agency interactions for assigned products, and guide cross-functional teams on compliance requirements.

$78K–$105K CAD RAC recommended CTA/NDS experience Agency meetings
3

Senior Level · 5–10 Years

Senior Specialist / Manager, Regulatory Affairs

Develop regulatory strategy for new drug programs, mentor junior staff, manage agency relationships, and provide regulatory guidance to leadership and the broader business.

$105K–$140K CAD RAC required Global submissions People management
4

Leadership · 10+ Years

Director / VP, Regulatory Affairs

Own the full regulatory strategy for an entire portfolio. Lead a team of RA professionals, interface directly with executive leadership, and represent your company at industry and agency forums.

$140K–$220K+ CAD Executive presence Portfolio strategy Team leadership

Pro Tip

Don't overlook contract research organizations (CROs) and regulatory consulting firms as early-career launchpads. The variety of therapeutic areas and submission types you encounter at a CRO can fast-track your expertise significantly compared to staying in one company.

06

Interview Tips

Landing an RA interview is one thing — walking out with an offer is another. Here are the most important things to focus on when preparing for a regulatory affairs interview at any level:

01

Know the Regulatory Framework

Interviewers will almost always ask about ICH guidelines (especially ICH E6, Q8, Q9, Q10), the CTD structure, and the difference between a CTA (Clinical Trial Application), NDS (New Drug Submission), NDS/SNDS, and administrative new drug submission. Know these cold and be ready to explain them simply.

02

Bring Real Examples (Even From School)

Use the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) for behavioral questions. If you are entry-level, draw from coursework, internships, lab projects, or research — interviewers know you are building experience.

03

Research the Company's Pipeline

Review the company's current drug programs on their website and ClinicalTrials.gov before your interview. Being able to mention their specific programs shows genuine interest and initiative — it stands out immediately.

04

Ask Smart Questions About Strategy

Ask things like: "How does the RA team get involved in early-phase development?" or "What does the agency relationship look like for your lead programs?" Questions about strategy show you think at the level they're hiring for.

05

Show Your Attention to Detail

Regulatory Affairs is a field where accuracy is everything. Make sure your resume is flawless, your cover letter is tailored, and during interviews, demonstrate that you think carefully and catch the details others miss.

06

Don't Be Afraid to Say "I'll Learn"

Especially at entry level, honesty about your current knowledge paired with clear enthusiasm to learn is respected. Saying "I haven't worked with eCTD publishing directly, but I've studied the structure and I'm committed to getting up to speed quickly" is a strong answer.

Growth Outlook

Statistics Canada and industry reports project strong growth for regulatory specialists across healthcare and pharmaceutical sectors, driven by the increasing complexity of drug development, expansion of biologics and cell/gene therapies, and rising global submission requirements. Canada's thriving biotech and pharma hubs in Ontario, Quebec, and BC are fuelling sustained demand for RA professionals. Regulatory expertise will only become more valuable as the Canadian pipeline grows more complex.

Resources to Get Started

You don't need to navigate this journey alone. These are some of the most helpful organizations, platforms, and tools for anyone building a career in Regulatory Affairs:

RAPS (raps.org)

The global home for RA professionals. Certifications, news, events, and job board — widely used by Canadian RA professionals.

Health Canada Drug Submissions (canada.ca)

Health Canada's official guidance on drug submissions, CTAs, and the NDS/NOC process — essential reading for every Canadian RA professional.

ICH Guidelines (ich.org)

The definitive source for international harmonization guidelines every RA professional must know.

Innovative Medicines Canada (innovativemedicines.ca)

Canada's research-based pharmaceutical industry association. Key resource for regulatory policy, industry standards, and career insights.

LinkedIn RA Groups

Search "Regulatory Affairs" groups and follow professionals in the field for real-time job posts and insights.
