01

What Is Market Access?

Market Access is the function responsible for ensuring that a new drug, once approved, can actually reach the patients who need it — at a price that payers (provincial drug plans and pharmacy benefit managers, government health programs) are willing to cover.

In Canada, market access requires navigating one of the most complex payer landscapes in the world. After receiving a Notice of Compliance (NOC) from Health Canada, a drug must undergo a health technology assessment through CADTH's Common Drug Review (CDR) or pan-Canadian Oncology Drug Review (pCODR). This is followed by price negotiation through the pCPA (pan-Canadian Pharmaceutical Alliance), and then individual listings with provincial formulary committees across the country. Each step involves different decision-makers, evidence requirements, and timelines.

Market Access professionals develop pricing strategies, craft value dossiers, submit to Health Technology Assessment (HTA) bodies like CADTH (Canadian Agency for Drugs and Technologies in Health), and build relationships with provincial formulary decision-makers. They bridge the gap between a drug's clinical value and its commercial viability.

Without effective market access strategy, even the most effective medicines can be inaccessible to patients due to coverage denials or affordability barriers.

Quick Fact

A drug can be fully approved by Health Canada and still be inaccessible to most patients if provincial drug plans refuse to cover it or place it on a non-preferred formulary tier. Market Access professionals are the ones who prevent that outcome — making this role just as vital as the science behind the drug itself.

Market Access professionals work closely with research scientists, clinical teams, quality assurance, medical affairs, and legal — making this one of the most cross-functional and intellectually stimulating careers in the industry. No two days look the same.

02

Why Choose Market Access?

If you are drawn to strategy, policy, economics, and the science of healthcare all at once, Market Access offers a career path unlike any other in pharma. Here is why professionals choose this field — and why it is growing faster than ever.

High Strategic Influence

Market Access professionals sit at the intersection of science, economics, and policy — with direct influence on product launch success and patient outcomes.

Premium Compensation

Market Access is among the highest-paying non-clinical functions in pharma, especially at director and above levels where strategic impact is highest.

Intellectual Diversity

This field uniquely combines health economics, real-world evidence, public policy, and commercial strategy — keeping your work intellectually rich and varied.

Growing Importance

PMPRB reforms and the growing influence of pCPA negotiations have made Canadian market access expertise more valuable than ever — driving exceptional demand for talent who understand the full federal and provincial access landscape.

03

Skills You'll Need

A strong Market Access professional blends scientific literacy with economic reasoning, policy awareness, and persuasive communication. You do not need a PhD in economics — but you do need to understand value and speak the language of payers.

Health Economics & Outcomes Research (HEOR)

Understanding how to measure and communicate the economic value of a treatment — the backbone of every market access strategy.

Value Dossier Development

Building comprehensive evidence packages that present a drug's clinical and economic value to payers and formulary committees.

Payer Landscape Analysis

Mapping coverage policies across Medicare, Medicaid, commercial insurers, and PBMs to anticipate and overcome access barriers.

HTA Submission Strategy

Preparing and submitting dossiers to Health Technology Assessment bodies like NICE, ICER, and G-BA for formal value reviews.

Pricing & Reimbursement

Developing global and US pricing strategies that balance commercial objectives with payer acceptance and patient affordability.

Government Affairs & Policy Navigation

Engaging with policy stakeholders, understanding legislative dynamics, and positioning your company favorably in drug pricing debates.

Bonus Skill

Familiarity with cost-effectiveness modeling (Markov models, budget impact analyses) using tools like Microsoft Excel, R, or TreeAge is a significant differentiator. Several online courses through ISPOR and Coursera cover health economic modeling for beginners.

Certifications That Open Doors

Market Access does not require a specific license to practice, but the right credentials signal credibility to employers and sharpen your core expertise. Whether you are entering from a science, policy, or business background, there is a credential pathway for you.

Offered by ISPOR, this credential covers health economics and outcomes research training and is widely recognized across pharma, biotech, and consulting — with no prior experience required.

Career Pathway

Market Access offers a clearly defined career ladder with strong earning potential at every stage. Most professionals enter at the analyst level and build payer expertise over time before moving into strategy, leadership, and executive roles. Here is what the trajectory typically looks like:

1

Entry Level · 0–2 Years

Market Access Analyst / HEOR Analyst

Support senior market access teams with payer landscape research, value communication materials, and literature reviews. Build your foundation in health economics and payer dynamics.

$72K–$95K BS/MS degree Payer landscape research Value communication support
2

Mid-Level · 2–5 Years

Market Access Manager / Associate Director

Lead payer negotiations, own value dossier development, and manage HTA submissions for assigned products. Build direct payer relationships and cross-functional launch team involvement.

$95K–$125K Payer negotiations Value dossier lead HTA submissions
3

Senior Level · 5–10 Years

Director, Market Access

Own launch strategy for key products, lead cross-functional market access teams, and sit on payer advisory boards. Develop integrated access strategies that span pricing, coverage, and patient support.

$125K–$160K Launch strategy Cross-functional leadership Payer advisory boards
4

Leadership · 10+ Years

VP Market Access / Head of Access & Pricing

Own global pricing strategy, represent the company at the executive level, shape drug development decisions from a market access lens, and lead government affairs engagement.

$160K–$220K+ Global pricing strategy Executive team Government affairs

Pro Tip

Getting experience on the payer side — at a pharmacy benefit manager (PBM), health plan, or consulting firm — is one of the most powerful career moves in market access. Understanding how formulary decisions are made from the inside gives you a perspective that is nearly impossible to replicate from pharma alone, and it is extremely valuable to employers.

06

Interview Tips

Market Access interviews test both technical knowledge and strategic thinking. Interviewers want to know that you understand the payer environment, can think through complex access scenarios, and are genuinely invested in ensuring patients can access treatment. Here is how to prepare:

01

Know the US Payer Landscape

Understand the difference between Medicare Part B and Part D, Medicaid, commercial insurers, and PBMs — and how each one makes coverage and formulary decisions. This is foundational knowledge for any market access role.

02

Be Ready to Discuss a Drug Launch Scenario

How would you position a $200K per year oncology drug to a formulary committee? Be ready to articulate a value story, anticipate payer objections, and discuss the evidence you would bring to the table.

03

Understand HTA Bodies

Know what NICE, ICER, and G-BA are, how they evaluate drugs, and what QALYs (Quality-Adjusted Life Years) mean. Demonstrating fluency in international HTA processes signals that you think beyond the US market.

04

Demonstrate Your Health Economics Fundamentals

Know the difference between a cost-effectiveness analysis and a budget impact analysis — and when each is used. Interviewers at director level will often test this distinction directly.

05

Research the Company's Current Access Challenges

Look into pricing scrutiny they may be facing, Inflation Reduction Act implications for their portfolio, or formulary exclusions affecting their key brands. Showing awareness of their real-world challenges demonstrates serious preparation.

06

Ask About the Company's Patient Support Programs

Asking about co-pay assistance programs, hub services, and patient access programs shows that you understand market access as a complete patient journey — not just a pricing and coverage exercise.

Growth Outlook

Canada's drug pricing reforms — including PMPRB regulatory changes and the growing influence of pCPA negotiations — are reshaping market access strategy at every company with a Canadian portfolio. The complexity of navigating Health Canada approval, CADTH health technology assessment, pCPA price negotiation, and individual provincial formulary listings simultaneously makes experienced Canadian market access professionals among the most valued in the industry. The demand for talent with CADTH submission experience and provincial formulary expertise is particularly strong.

Resources to Get Started

You do not need to navigate this journey alone. These are some of the most helpful organizations, platforms, and communities for anyone building a career in Market Access:

ISPOR (ispor.org)

The global home for health economics and outcomes research professionals — certifications, journals, and job boards.

ICER (icer.org)

Read ICER reports to understand how US value assessments work in practice — essential reading for market access professionals.

AMCP (amcp.org)

Academy of Managed Care Pharmacy — a key resource for payer and formulary strategy, with robust professional development offerings.

KFF Health System Tracker (healthsystemtracker.org)

Track US drug pricing policy in real time — an essential tool for staying current on the payer and policy environment.

LinkedIn Market Access Groups

Search "Pharmaceutical Market Access" and "HEOR" for active job posts, policy discussions, and professional connections.
